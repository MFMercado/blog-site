DIY Samgyeopsal

Korean food at the comfort of your home.

Posted by faye.mercado on May 28, 2022

With the popularity of Korean food here in the Philippines, I got hooked up particularly with Samgyeopsal. The variety of meats and many choices of side dishes or banchan that accompanies the main dish or the Samgyeopsal itself makes my heart and equally my stomach very happy.

Having Samgyeopsal at home has its pros and cons. Some of the cons are the tedious preparation of the dishes (if you're like me who makes them from scratch) and the enormous pile of plates and pots to wash after eating (haha). The pros however are enjoying the fruit or hard labor of preparation and having it in the comfort of your home. No time pressure whatsoever and freedom to make any of the banchans that you like. It's also cheaper if you are planning to make big batches for some occasions.

Don't get me wrong, I don't eat Samgyeopsal everyday. I eat occasionally, but when I do, I mostly make the dishes myself. I like doing it because it gives me flexibity in the taste and level of pungency of the food.

Journey before Samgy day

Here are the typical preparations that I do a day or two before Samgy day (the day when you are ready to grill and eat).

I buy precut meats and season a portion of it if I am making different flavored cuts of meat like bulgogi and/or spicy. As for the banchans, which I like making from scratch, I look up the recipes from the web and buy the ingredients in the market.

I have listed my favorite banchans here with the link to their recipes that I follow if you want to make them for yourself too.

  • Kimchi
  • Kongnamul Muchim (Seasoned Soybean Sprouts)
  • Oi Muchim (Spicy Cucumber Salad)
  • Gamja Jorim (Braised Potatoes)
  • Goguma Mattang (Candied Sweet Potatoes)
  • Gyeran Mari (Egg Roll)
  • Eomuk Bokkeum (Stir-fried Fish Cake)
  • Pa Muchim (Scallion Salad)

Also, don't forget the cooking equipments! Make sure you have your grill well maintained and ready for cooking.

Samgy day: Fruit of tedious labor


Go for it. You deserve this. (wink)

Finally, after the hard work comes the celebration. It's Samgy day and you get to enjoy the food that you elaborately cooked. Savour it and fill up your stomach because you'll have a sink full of dirty dishes afterwards (haha just kidding. . .or maybe not 😆).


Mashikeh-mogoseyo!
